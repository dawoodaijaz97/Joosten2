Joosten Black

”So the darkness shall be the light”

From 2006-2012 Joosten gave weekly Sunday yoga classes in a traditional Japanese wooden temple Kannon-do (temple of Avalokitesvara) at Chizanso, Hayama Japan. The experience during these meditative sessions in this unique setting over many years, has strongly influenced his view on light and shadow.

“...we find beauty not in the thing itself but in the patterns of shadows, the light and the
darkness, that one thing against another creates. (...) Were it not for shadows, there
would be no beauty.”
What lies within the darkness one cannot distinguish, but the palm senses the gentle
movements of the liquid, vapour rises from within, forming droplets on the rim, and the
fragrance carried upon the vapour brings a delicate anticipation (...)
A moment of mystery, it might almost be called, a moment of trance.”
(Junichiro Tanizaki, In praise of shadows)

“I said to my soul, be still, and let the dark come upon you
Which shall be the darkness of God. As, in a theatre,
The lights are extinguished, for the scene to be changed
With a hollow rumble of wings, with a movement of darkness on darkness,
And we know that the hills and the trees, the distant panorama
And the bold imposing facade are all being rolled away-
Or as, when an underground train, in the tube, stops too long between stations
And the conversation rises and slowly fades into silence
And you see behind every face the mental emptiness deepen
Leaving only the growing terror of nothing to think about;
Or when, under ether, the mind is conscious but conscious of nothing-

I said to my soul, be still, and wait without hope
For hope would be hope for the wrong thing; wait without love,
For love would be love of the wrong thing; there is yet faith
But the faith and the love and the hope are all in the waiting.
Wait without thought, for you are not ready for thought:
So the darkness shall be the light, and the stillness the dancing.
Whisper of running streams, and winter lightning.
The wild thyme unseen and the wild strawberry,
The laughter in the garden, echoed ecstasy
Not lost, but requiring, pointing to the agony
Of death and birth.”
(T.S. Eliot, Four Quartets - East Cocker, third quartet)

With his recent photography and light works Joosten explores a darker expanse where the light reflects from within.
After his move from Japan to Germany in 2013 he started to experiment with black & white paper cut-outs and cast resin shapes placed into reliefs, projecting light from behind the paper and rain shapes.
The photographic works were created by projecting a white LED light from behind the paper cut-out planes, while capturing the image with a moving camera. The light boxes consist of a black front panel hung on the wall, in which a negative silhouette has been cut out by a digital laser cutter.
Behind this front panel, a semi-transparent plate is mounted. A white LED light is installed behind the panels into a black frame so that the light only shines through the cut-out openings of the front layer. The light source is connected to a small microcontroller that will move the intensity of the light as if it slowly breaths in and out, as a light source that slowly appear and dissolves.

"Life is the light that shines in the darkness!"
(Hayao Miyazaki, Nausicaä of the valley of the wind, Vol.7-2 The Crypt)

Creating an infinite black space in which all life appearances begins and end. A silent empty void in which sinuous light whisperings emerge, slowly appearing and dissolving on the brink of existence. The light breathing, in and out. A caress, a sensual dance, embracing the night. A signature coming to life, a scripture fading away.

Like the last moment before falling asleep, when we have laid down our clothes, our various roles and identities, we dissolve into absolute darkness. Or reversely, as the first instant in which we seem to reappear right before awakening. An infinite empty blackness in which all life appearances begin and end, the background of our being.

“One does not become enlightened by imagining figures of light, but by making the darkness conscious. "
(C.G. Jung “The Philosophical Tree” (1945) In Collected Works13: Alchemical Studies)

“To confront a person with his shadow is to show him his own light. Once one has
experienced a few times what it is like to stand judgingly between the opposites,
one begins to understand what is meant by the self. Anyone who perceives his shadow and his
light simultaneously sees himself from two sides and thus gets in the middle.”
(C.G.Jung “Good and Evil in Analytical Psychology” (1959) In Collected Works 10. Civilization in Transition.)
